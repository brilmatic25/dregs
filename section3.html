Cantilever Hummingbird (Dark Differance)

Black folks living is a caged bird singing,

but we must consider how the hum of the hummingbird necessarily becomes the scream of a murder of crow, a craw, a crock [crock (noun): one that is broken down; insincere or foolish talk; a complaining (social justice) hypochondriac], a crack which screams "do not step on me, or Ima have to hurt yo mama!"

Whether we "hum in the face of the unsayability of words" or holler at that which catches us by a tow is a question regarding the chosen frequency of our illegibility (infra-read or ultra-violent?) insofar as our musically performative, restive, resistive utterances can only ever be infelicitous, can only ever miss their mark.

Our impassioned response to the given order of things is thus a cantilevered bridge reaching helplessly (which is to say self-sufficiently) and haplessly into an open ocean of mut[e]ation; the phonic material speech act being firmly rooted in a diasporically understood experience of subjection, and its receiver (power) woefully lacking foundation, or more precisely being founded upon a judgemental cut or clipping of a primordial and ante-normative antenna. This is what is at stake when we say that objects can and do resist while also admitting the subaltern cannot speak.

Maybe this is what it sounds like to be a problem, to be cursed with the drama of a letter unsent. Forlorn, purloined, intercepted, returned to sender—ink drowned in tidal rage against any hope of knowability. Call unanswered, signal too high, demand without command, hummingbird in futile search of a well-kempt flower ("please leave The Message").

We are the un-sents, the dis-sents, the pre-sents, the nonsense. We are not designed to be delivered but are shared all over nonetheless. A tear in the world streams violently from a sunken eye. The wise old man points to the horizon of the event. His wrinkles grin with anticipation: "follow me and you gon' cross a bridge to nowhere…"

This thingliness I cannot deliver but which delivers me, this shadow prayer I am preaching to a choir that doesn't even know they hear me, is "the open obscurity of a field of study in a line of flight," the Obscure Us, that tree of life falling, under the gaze and over again.

We speak with eight tongues and eighteen temporalities. I've never (ever ever) heard this not be called gibberish.
